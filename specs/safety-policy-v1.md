# Safety Policy v1

## Status

Active for local demo.

## Purpose

Keep the application within communication-support boundaries. The system should help users explore possible interpretations and bridges without diagnosis, blame, normalization, or unsupported advice.

## Prohibited Or Sensitive Categories

### Diagnostic Framing

Avoid diagnosing people, naming disorders as conclusions, or implying clinical assessment.

### Clinical Labeling

Avoid labeling a person with clinical or psychiatric terms.

### Behavioral Normalization

Avoid framing one communication style as normal, abnormal, proper, or defective.

### Blame Assignment

Avoid deciding who is right, wrong, guilty, responsible, or at fault.

### Pathologizing Language

Avoid stigmatizing or dismissive terms such as lazy, crazy, manipulative, irrational, or overreacting.

### Harassment Or Abuse

Do not treat harassment, abuse, threats, or coercive conflict as ordinary communication mismatch.

### Emotional Risk

Do not treat self-harm, suicide, or immediate safety language as routine mediation.

### Out Of Scope Requests

Do not provide legal, medical, HR, clinical, or specialized educational determinations.

## Required Response Posture

- Use possible interpretations.
- Support mutual clarification.
- Preserve uncertainty.
- Encourage appropriate human support for high-risk or out-of-scope cases.
